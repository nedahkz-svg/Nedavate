AI Training Readiness Checklist

Ten honest checks. Tick the ones that are true for your team today. The boxes you can't tick are exactly where training is leaking time, money, and people.

01

Your onboarding has a defined outcome — not just content

New hires reach a measurable level of capability by a set date, and you know what that date is.

02

You can name the cost of your current training gaps

Turnover, ramp time, errors, rework — you can point to a number, not a feeling.

03

Training is built for the role, not one-size-fits-all

A new manager and a new engineer don't sit through the identical generic course.

04

You measure transfer, not just completion

Success is "did behaviour change on the job?" — not "did they click to the end?"

05

Your content is reusable and easy to update

When a policy or product changes, you can update the module in hours — not rebuild it.

06

Your team knows which AI tools are approved — and how to use them

There's a clear, practical answer, not a quiet free-for-all or a blanket ban.

07

Repetitive support questions are handled, not re-answered daily

An assistant, knowledge base, or bot deflects the "where do I find…?" questions.

08

Compliance training is something people finish, not endure

It's scenario-based and relevant — not 45 slides clicked through at year-end.

09

You have capacity to ship learning on deadline

When a build is needed fast, you have the skills or a partner to deliver — reliably.

10

Your training is ready to scale with you

It's designed for where the organisation is going, not just where it is today.

Your score

  • 8–10 ticked: You're ahead of most. The opportunity is sharpening measurement and adding AI where it compounds.
  • 4–7 ticked: Solid foundation, real leaks. Fixing 2–3 of these usually pays for itself fast.
  • 0–3 ticked: Training is likely costing you more than you think. Start with onboarding and transfer — the highest-leverage fixes.
